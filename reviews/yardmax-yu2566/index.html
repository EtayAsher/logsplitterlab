YARDMAX 25-Ton Full Beam Gas Log Splitter Review

Model YU2566 · Published 2026-07-20 · Updated 2026-07-20 · Published by LogSplitterLab

This is a specification-based research summary, not a first-hand test. It compares manufacturer and retailer specifications against the factors homeowners typically care about. If we ever physically test this model, this notice will be replaced with first-hand observations clearly separated from manufacturer claims.

Verdict

A 25-ton gas splitter with the fastest cycle time of the machines we've checked, aimed at higher-volume splitting.

Best for

Users processing larger volumes of wood who want to minimize time spent waiting on the ram between logs.

Not ideal for

Anyone who wants to avoid gas engine upkeep, or needs a quiet machine to use near neighbors.

Key strengths

  • 9.7-second cycle time is the fastest among the splitters in our current data
  • Handles logs up to 26 in. long, the longest rated capacity we've checked
  • Horizontal and vertical operation with DOT-approved tires for towing

Key limitations

  • Gas engine requires the same routine maintenance as any small engine
  • 25-ton rating is slightly below Champion's 27-ton unit for the very densest rounds
  • Full-beam gas splitters of this size are heavier to maneuver by hand than electric models

Usability considerations

Two-handed operation is standard; the manufacturer's U-beam frame is designed to resist bending under repeated heavy use.

Maintenance considerations

Follow YARDMAX's manual for Briggs & Stratton engine service intervals and hydraulic reservoir checks (4.2-gallon capacity). Fluid types and service intervals vary by model — always follow the manufacturer's manual rather than a generic schedule. See our maintenance guide for general upkeep concepts.

Safety considerations

Operate this splitter only as described in the manufacturer's manual. Keep hands clear of the wedge and ram path, split one log at a time unless the machine is explicitly rated otherwise, and wear eye protection and sturdy footwear. See our general safety guidance — it does not replace the manufacturer's instructions for this specific model.

Compared with a realistic alternative

Compared to the Champion 100424, this YARDMAX model splits faster per cycle (9.7s vs. 11s) but at slightly lower tonnage (25T vs. 27T) — worth weighing against the density of wood you split most. Read the Champion Power Equipment 100424 summary.
