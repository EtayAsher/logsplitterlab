Champion 27-Ton Log Splitter Review

Model 100424 · Published 2026-07-20 · Updated 2026-07-20 · Published by LogSplitterLab

This is a specification-based research summary, not a first-hand test. It compares manufacturer and retailer specifications against the factors homeowners typically care about. If we ever physically test this model, this notice will be replaced with first-hand observations clearly separated from manufacturer claims.

Verdict

A conventional 27-ton gas splitter built to be towed between sites and to handle repeated full-cord sessions rather than occasional light use.

Best for

Rural property owners processing several cords a year who need to move the machine around a property, or split wherever the wood is rather than at a single fixed spot.

Not ideal for

Anyone who wants to avoid small-engine maintenance, or needs a quiet machine to run near a house in the evening.

Key strengths

  • DOT-approved tow package rated to 45 mph, so it can go where the wood is
  • Handles logs up to 24 in. long and 100 lb, covering most residential firewood rounds
  • Horizontal and vertical operation, useful for rounds too heavy to lift onto a beam

Key limitations

  • Gas engine requires periodic oil changes and off-season fuel stabilization
  • Cycle time varies by exact model number within Champion's 27-ton line — confirm the number on the box matches 100424 before comparing specs
  • Louder and produces exhaust, unlike an electric alternative

Usability considerations

Two-handed control operation is standard on this class of splitter; expect to brace the machine using its stabilizer legs on uneven ground before operating.

Maintenance considerations

Follow Champion's manual for engine oil type/interval and hydraulic fluid level checks. Inspect the tow hitch and tires before transporting. Fluid types and service intervals vary by model — always follow the manufacturer's manual rather than a generic schedule. See our maintenance guide for general upkeep concepts.

Safety considerations

Operate this splitter only as described in the manufacturer's manual. Keep hands clear of the wedge and ram path, split one log at a time unless the machine is explicitly rated otherwise, and wear eye protection and sturdy footwear. See our general safety guidance — it does not replace the manufacturer's instructions for this specific model.

Compared with a realistic alternative

Compared to the YARDMAX YU2566, this Champion model handles slightly shorter maximum log length (24 in. vs. 26 in.) but is towable up to highway speed; check both spec tables above before choosing. Read the YARDMAX YU2566 summary.
