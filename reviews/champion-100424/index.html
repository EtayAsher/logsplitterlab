Verdict

A conventional 27-ton gas splitter built to be towed between sites and to handle repeated full-cord sessions rather than occasional light use.

Best for

Rural property owners processing several cords a year who need to move the machine around a property, or split wherever the wood is rather than at a single fixed spot.

Not ideal for

Anyone who wants to avoid small-engine maintenance, or needs a quiet machine to run near a house in the evening.

Key strengths

  • DOT-approved tow package rated to 45 mph, so it can go where the wood is
  • Handles logs up to 24 in. long and 100 lb, covering most residential firewood rounds
  • Horizontal and vertical operation, useful for rounds too heavy to lift onto a beam

Key limitations

  • Gas engine requires periodic oil changes and off-season fuel stabilization
  • Cycle time varies by exact model number within Champion's 27-ton line — confirm the number on the box matches 100424 before comparing specs
  • Louder and produces exhaust, unlike an electric alternative

Power and expected workload

Suited to homeowners and rural property owners processing multiple cords a year, not occasional weekend splitting.

Cycle time

An 11-second cycle time (per Champion's spec for this model number) is competitive with other gas splitters in this tonnage class, though the YARDMAX YU2566 posts a faster 9.7-second cycle — worth weighing if speed matters more to you than towing at highway speed.

Log size considerations

Rated for logs up to 24 in. long and 100 lb. Rounds longer or heavier than that should be cut down before splitting.

Portability

DOT-approved wheels and a 2-inch ball hitch coupler rated to 45 mph make this a genuinely towable machine, per Champion's specifications — confirm your tow vehicle and trailer hitch are rated accordingly before towing on public roads.

Setup and storage

Requires assembly of the tow tongue and wheel hardware per Champion's manual on first setup. For storage, retract the ram fully and, if storing over winter, stabilize or drain fuel per the engine manufacturer's guidance.

Noise and fumes

As a gas splitter, expect engine noise and exhaust — plan to operate it away from windows or close neighbors. No electrical hookup is needed.

Usability

Two-handed control operation is standard on this class of splitter; expect to brace the machine using its stabilizer legs on uneven ground before operating.

Maintenance expectations

Follow Champion's manual for engine oil type/interval and hydraulic fluid level checks. Inspect the tow hitch and tires before transporting. Fluid types and service intervals vary by model — always follow the manufacturer's manual rather than a generic schedule. See our maintenance guide for general upkeep concepts.

Safety notes

Operate this splitter only as described in the manufacturer's manual. Keep hands clear of the wedge and ram path, split one log at a time unless the machine is explicitly rated otherwise, and wear eye protection and sturdy footwear. See our general safety guidance — it does not replace the manufacturer's instructions for this specific model.

Who should buy it

Choose this model if you need to move a splitter between work sites on a rural property, or process enough wood that 27 tons of consistent force matters more than a quieter alternative.

Who should choose another product

If you split lighter volumes near your house and want to avoid engine noise and maintenance, an electric model like the WEN 56207 or Boss Industrial ES7T20 is a better fit. See our Best Gas Log Splitters roundup for other verified options.

Compared with a realistic alternative

Compared to the YARDMAX YU2566, this Champion model handles slightly shorter maximum log length (24 in. vs. 26 in.) but is towable up to a confirmed highway speed; check both spec tables above before choosing. Read the YARDMAX YU2566 summary.
